# Assessment & the Skill Map

How Kaelyn's Adaptive Curriculum finds her real starting rung in each strand and then tracks growth, continuously and without ever turning into a high-stakes test. Read with `README.md` §2 (mastery) and §6 (progress).

> The whole point of placement here is the opposite of a typical test: we are not checking whether she has gaps at her grade. We are finding **how far up each ladder she already is** so we can teach her something new on day one.

---

## 1. Placement: find her entry rung, per strand

Placement is **playful and low-stakes** ("show me what you can do!"), done once at the start and then re-run lightly for any strand that turns out mis-placed. Each strand file carries its own probe; this is the order and the decision rule.

| Strand | Probe (from the strand file) | Expected entry (to confirm) |
|---|---|---|
| 📖 Reading | Read a short leveled chapter-book passage aloud + talk about it (retell, one "why" question) | fluency + literal/inferential comprehension (mid ladder), not the bottom |
| 🔤 Word Study | Quick single-syllable check, then read/spell a few multisyllable + affixed words | confirm single-syllable floor is solid, start at multisyllable / morphology |
| ✍️ Writing | Compose **orally** (scribed) so her hand never caps it; separately, a brief private handwriting peek | composition mid-ladder, transcription near the bottom (the asynchrony) |
| 🔢 Math | Confirm +/− fluency + place value, then equal-groups / array / a multiplication question | start at multiplication/division, frontier likely at fractions |

**Decision rule (per strand):**
- Breezes the probe → place **one rung higher** and offer the stretch immediately.
- Solid but working → place **at** the probed rung.
- Struggles → step **down one rung** and confirm. (For Kaelyn this should be rare except in Writing-transcription, which is *expected* to sit low and is fine.)

The result is her **starting skill map**: a rung per strand, set independently. She will, by design, start at very different heights across strands (high reading/math, low writing-transcription). That spread is correct, not a problem to fix.

---

## 2. Ongoing assessment is just the work

After placement there are no separate tests. The **agentic tutor reads mastery continuously** from the activities she does (README §2):

- **Mastery gate (default):** a skill goes *solid* when she succeeds **independently on ≥ 4 of 5 fresh items across ≥ 2 different days**. Below that it's *emerging* and she gets more varied practice. The tutor can raise the bar for foundational skills and lower the reps when she's clearly flying.
- **Advance per strand.** Mastering a rung advances her in that strand only. A strong reading day never moves the writing ladder.
- **Re-place when signals say so.** Several too-easy sessions in a row → the tutor jumps her up a rung (and logs why, parent-visible). Repeated genuine struggle → step back one rung and rebuild. This keeps her always at the edge of her ability: challenged, not frustrated, not bored.
- **Writing is gated on ideas, not hands.** The composition gate measures sentence/structure/organization quality (scribed or typed is fine); transcription advances on its own gentle gate. They are never conflated.

---

## 3. The skill map (parent-visible)

Every skill (every rung) shows one of three honest states, per strand:

- **not-yet** — not introduced or not started. Neutral, never "behind." (Color + label, never a red X.)
- **emerging** — taught and practicing; not yet independent.
- **solid** — independent and retained (passed the gate).

The parent dashboard shows this as **four independent ladders filling at their own rates**, with **specific evidence** under each, for example:

- "Reads 2-syllable vowel-team words on her own" (`word.vowel-teams.multisyllable` → solid)
- "Multiplies within 5 using arrays; building 2s/5s/10s facts" (`math.mult.arrays` solid, `math.mult.facts` emerging)
- "Composes a 3-sentence story orally; types a paragraph about whales" (`writing.compose.narrative` emerging) — shown right next to "Forms lowercase letters in the first stroke group" (`writing.transcription.letter-formation` emerging), so the asynchrony is **visible and normalized**, not hidden.

No single number, no grade level stamped on the child, no vanity score. Specific, current, true.

---

## 4. What Kaelyn sees (progress as motivation)

For her, growth is concrete and earned (README §6), never a manipulative streak:

- a **ladder that fills** in each strand as she masters rungs,
- a **portfolio** of the pieces she has composed (her real work, kept),
- **badges** for mastered fact sets and morphemes (e.g. "×5 facts," "the prefix *re-*," "vowel teams"),
- a **before/after** she can see (a passage she couldn't read in June and can in August; a story she dictated then later typed then later wrote).

Visible progress at the edge of her ability is the engine. The assessment exists only to keep her there.
