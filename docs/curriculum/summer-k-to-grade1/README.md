# Summer Bridge: Kindergarten → 1st Grade (stretch to 2nd)

**Program 01 of Kaelyn's Academy** · A 10-week, play-balanced summer curriculum
**Learner profile:** Just finished kindergarten · on-track (knows letters & sounds, counts, simple addition, building early reading) · ~5–6 years old
**Goal:** Walk into 1st grade confident and ahead — with several **2nd-grade "stretch" skills** already in reach.
**Suggested dates:** Mon **Jun 15 – Aug 21, 2026** (10 weeks). Start date is flexible; the week numbers matter more than the calendar.

---

## 1. Philosophy: rigorous *and* joyful

A 6-year-old does not need "summer school." She needs **short, daily, high-quality skill-building wrapped in play, story, and discovery.** This plan is built on five principles:

1. **Little and often beats long and rare.** ~60–90 minutes of focused "learning blocks" per day, broken into 10–20 minute chunks, 5 days a week. Weekends are light/optional.
2. **Decode, don't memorize.** Reading is built on systematic phonics (sounds → blends → patterns), not guessing. Math is built on *understanding* (manipulatives, number sense) before drilling facts.
3. **Theme-based and cross-curricular.** Each week has a theme (Ocean, Space, Dinosaurs…) that ties reading, writing, math, science, and art together so skills feel purposeful, not isolated.
4. **Stamina and self-regulation are kindergarten-readiness skills too.** We deliberately build focus, "I can do hard things" persistence, and independent work habits — the social-emotional muscles 1st grade demands.
5. **Stretch, never strain.** Every week includes a **2nd-grade reach** target. We *offer* it; we never force it. If she's flying, lean in. If a week is hard, repeat it.

> **The one rule that matters most:** Keep it warm. If a session turns into a fight, stop, shrink it, or turn it into a game. A child who *likes* learning in June will outpace a child who was drilled into resentment.

## 2. What "ready for 1st grade (and reaching toward 2nd)" actually means

These are the concrete targets this summer builds toward, aligned to Common Core (CA-adopted) ELA & Math standards. Detailed can-do checklists are in [`assessment-checklists.md`](./assessment-checklists.md).

### Reading & Language
| By end of summer (1st-grade ready) | 2nd-grade stretch |
|---|---|
| Knows all letter sounds incl. digraphs (sh, ch, th) | Reads with expression & phrasing |
| Blends & reads CVC, blends, and silent-e words | Reads vowel teams & r-controlled words |
| Reads ~100 high-frequency (sight) words | Reads ~150+ sight words incl. Grade-2 list |
| Reads simple decodable books, retells the story | Reads early chapter books (Frog and Toad) |
| Writes a complete sentence (capital, spaces, period) | Writes 3–5 sentence stories with detail |

### Math
| By end of summer (1st-grade ready) | 2nd-grade stretch |
|---|---|
| Counts to 120; reads/writes numbers to 100 | Skip-counts by 2s, 5s, 10s; counts to 1000 |
| Add & subtract within 20 (strategies, then fluency to 10) | Two-digit add/subtract (no regrouping → regrouping) |
| Understands tens & ones (place value) | Hundreds, tens, ones; compares 3-digit numbers |
| Tells time to the hour & half-hour | Tells time to 5 minutes; counts coins |
| Names 2D/3D shapes; halves & fourths | Arrays & repeated addition (intro to multiplication) |

## 3. The daily rhythm

A repeatable flow makes the day predictable (kids thrive on this) and easy for any caregiver to run. Total focused time ≈ **75 minutes**, the rest is play, outdoors, and life.

| Block | Time | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| ☀️ **Morning Meeting** | 10 min | Calendar, weather, "question of the day," today's plan. Counting/skip-count warm-up. Builds routine + number sense. |
| 📖 **Reading & Phonics** | 20 min | New sound/pattern → word work → read a decodable/leveled book together. (See weekly phonics focus.) |
| 🏃 **Brain Break** | 5–10 min | Movement: GoNoodle, dance, animal walks, "Simon Says." Resets attention. |
| 🔢 **Math** | 20 min | Hands-on concept (manipulatives) → 1 short practice → 1 game. |
| ✏️ **Writing / Journal** | 10–15 min | Handwriting warm-up + theme journal prompt (draw + label → sentence). |
| 🔬 **Theme / Project / Read-aloud** | 15–30 min | Science/art/STEM tied to the week's theme + a cozy read-aloud. The "fun anchor" of the day. |
| 🌙 **Bedtime read-aloud** | 15 min | *Every night.* Single biggest predictor of reading success. You read; she follows along / chimes in. |

**Tips**
- Do the hard/focus blocks (reading, math) in the **morning** when attention is freshest.
- Use a **visual schedule** (icons she can check off) — independence + pride.
- A **timer** ("we read until the sand runs out") turns transitions into a game, not a battle.

## 5. Scope & sequence (why the order is the order)

**Phonics arc** — each pattern builds on the last, and decodable books are chosen to match:
`short vowels/CVC → digraphs → beginning blends → ending blends → silent-e → vowel teams → r-controlled → diphthongs → inflectional endings & syllables → multisyllable review.`

**Sight-word arc** — Dolch lists, ~6–8 new words/week, always reviewing prior words. Solidify Pre-Primer/Primer (most already known) → master Grade 1 → introduce Grade 2 (stretch). Exact weekly lists: [`sight-words.md`](./sight-words.md).

**Math arc** — concept before fluency, always with manipulatives first:
`counting & compare → place value (tens/ones) → addition → subtraction → fluency & word problems → two-digit addition [stretch] → measurement → time & money [stretch] → geometry & fractions → skip-counting & arrays [stretch].`

**Writing arc** — `letter formation & labeling → words & word spacing → one complete sentence → two related sentences → a 3-sentence "story" with a beginning/middle/end.`

## 6. Materials & resources

**Around the house / inexpensive:**
- Whiteboard + dry-erase markers (the single most-used tool — low-stakes, erasable practice)
- Counting objects (dried beans, LEGO, counting bears), dice (2), a deck of cards, dominoes
- **Ten-frames** + **base-ten blocks** (print free, or use LEGO: a 10-stud brick = "ten")
- Real coins (penny, nickel, dime, quarter), an **analog clock**, a ruler/tape measure
- Crayons/markers, pencils (chunky if needed), scissors, glue, index cards (for sight words)
- A 3-ring binder or folder = her "Academy Portfolio" (keep finished work; it shows growth)

**Books (the engine of the whole plan).** Aim for a weekly **book basket** of ~5 books tied to the theme + a steady diet of these readers:
- **Decodable / early readers:** BOB Books (sets 1–3), *Elephant & Piggie* (Mo Willems), *Frog and Toad* (Arnold Lobel — great 2nd-grade stretch), *Henry and Mudge*, *Pete the Cat*, *Fly Guy*.
- **Read-alouds (above her level, build vocabulary & comprehension):** *The Magic Tree House* #1 *Dinosaurs Before Dark*, *The Rainbow Fish*, *The Very Hungry Caterpillar*, *Last Stop on Market Street*, *Mae Among the Stars*. (Theme-specific titles listed each week.)
- **Library card.** Free, infinite, theme-matched. Reserve next week's basket online.

**Printables / digital (optional bridges until the Academy app is live):**
- Free ten-frames, number lines, handwriting sheets (search "free K phonics decodable" / "ten-frame printable").
- **Khan Academy Kids** (free, excellent, ad-free) and **Teach Your Monster to Read** (phonics) are solid interim apps. *These are placeholders — the adaptive practice will eventually live inside Kaelyn's Academy itself.*

## 7. Assessment & celebrating progress

Three light-touch checkpoints — never a "test," always a "show me what you can do!" game. Full printable checklists + a simple placement check are in [`assessment-checklists.md`](./assessment-checklists.md).

- **Week 1 — Baseline.** Find her real starting line (which sounds/sight words she knows, how high she counts, what math she can do). *Adjust the plan up or down based on this.*
- **Week 5 — Mid-summer.** Celebrate wins, spot anything to re-teach in weeks 6–10.
- **Week 10 — Final.** Compare to baseline. Make a **certificate**. Show her the before/after — kids are stunned and proud at how far they came.

**Track it visibly.** A sticker chart, a "words I can read" jar (drop in an index card per mastered sight word), a growth journal. Visible progress is its own motivation.

## 8. Parent playbook (how to actually run this)

- **Consistency > intensity.** 30 focused minutes daily for 10 weeks beats a 3-hour cram once a week.
- **Follow her energy.** Tired/cranky day? Do *only* the read-aloud and a movement game. That still counts.
- **Make it a game.** Sight-word hopscotch, "math facts while we drive," spelling words in shaving cream. Movement + learning = retention.
- **Resistance is data.** If she fights a task, it's usually **too hard** (shrink it) or **too easy/boring** (level up or make it silly). Rarely is it defiance.
- **Praise the effort, not the smarts.** "You worked *so hard* on that blend" > "you're so smart." Builds a growth mindset.
- **Screen-time:** keep app practice ≤20 min/day and co-play when you can. Hands-on and books are the main course.
- **Read aloud every single night**, no matter what else got skipped. It's non-negotiable and it's the highest-leverage thing you do.
- **Repeat a week** with no guilt if a skill isn't solid. The calendar is a suggestion; mastery is the goal.

## 9. How this becomes "Program 01" in Kaelyn's Academy

This curriculum is intentionally structured so it maps cleanly onto the platform we're building:

- **Program** → this whole 10-week bridge.
- **Unit** → each weekly theme.
- **Lesson** → each day.
- **Activity** → each block (phonics drill, math game, journal prompt, project), each tagged by **skill** (e.g. `phonics.digraphs`, `math.place-value`) and **standard**.
- The **agentic tutor** will use the skill tags + checkpoint results to recommend the next activity, adapt difficulty, and generate fresh practice — turning this static plan into a living, adaptive program.

So nothing here is throwaway: the day-by-day content, sight-word lists, and skill tags become the seed data and content model for the app's first program. See the platform design spec (next sub-project) for how the content model is derived from this.
