# Sight Words (High-Frequency Words)

**This file is the authoritative sight-word sequence** for the summer program (the weekly plans reference it). Words are drawn from the **Dolch lists**, the most common words in children's books — many can't be "sounded out" and must be recognized instantly so reading stays smooth.

## How to teach a sight word (5–10 min)
1. **See & say:** Show the word card. "This word is *said*. Say it: *said*."
2. **Spell & trace:** Spell aloud (s-a-i-d), trace in the air / in sand / on a whiteboard.
3. **Use it:** Say a sentence with it. Have her say one too.
4. **Find it:** Hunt for the word in a book or around the room.
5. **Review daily:** Keep a ring of cards; shuffle in old words. **3–5 new words a week is plenty.**

> **Calibrate to baseline.** A typical end-of-K student already reads many Pre-Primer/Primer words. During the Week 1 baseline, check these first; **skip the ones she knows and pull the next unknown word forward.** The goal is ~6 *new-to-her* words per week.

**Make it a game:** sight-word hopscotch, "swat the word" (fly swatter + cards on the floor), word hunt in the wild (signs, cereal boxes), memory/matching, and the **"Words I Can Read" jar** — drop in a card each time she masters one and watch the jar fill.

## Reference — full Dolch lists

Use these for the baseline check and for pulling replacement words. ✅ the ones she already knows.

**Pre-Primer (40):** a, and, away, big, blue, can, come, down, find, for, funny, go, help, here, I, in, is, it, jump, little, look, make, me, my, not, one, play, red, run, said, see, the, three, to, two, up, we, where, yellow, you

**Grade 1 (41):** after, again, an, any, as, ask, by, could, every, fly, from, give, going, had, has, her, him, his, how, just, know, let, live, may, of, old, once, open, over, put, round, some, stop, take, thank, them, then, think, walk, were, when

**Grade 2 (46):** always, around, because, been, before, best, both, buy, call, cold, does, don't, fast, first, five, found, gave, goes, green, its, made, many, off, or, pull, read, right, sing, sit, sleep, tell, their, these, those, upon, us, use, very, wash, which, why, wish, work, would, write, your

> **Note:** "Sight words" and "phonics" work together — many of these *are* decodable as her phonics grows (e.g., *with*, *that*, *fast*). Teach truly irregular ones (*said, what, the, was*) by sight; let her decode the rest as the patterns are introduced.
